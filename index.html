Hydraulic Press
Manan Rehlan

What is a Hydraulic Press?

A hydraulic press is a machine powered by fluids(liquid or gas) that is used to crush objects. I made a version at home.

Purpose

The purpose of this project is to demonstrate the efficiency of a hydraulic press in compacting trash and recycled materials so more objects can fit in the same basket or bin.

Hypothesis

My hypothesis is that incompressible fluids can be used for magnifying force by transferring it from a smaller piston to a larger piston in a confined system. According to Pascal's principle, the original pressure exerted on a small piston will produce an equal pressure on the large piston. However, because a large piston has a bigger area than the small one, it will produce a greater force.

Procedure
  1. Sketch the design of the model
  2. Measure wood & cut into pieces
  3. Drill holes & sand down wood
  4. Paint wood pieces
  5. Screw wood pieces together
  6. Glue piston to press head
  7. Fix 5 syringes inside frame
  8. Attach 1 syringe to push
  9. Attach Press Head
  10. Fix tubes & Valves
  11. Get water container
  12. Test model

Mateials Used
  • Wood (3): 5½cm x 5½cm x 1.9cm
  • Syringes (6) : 15cm,100ml
  • Connectors (2)
  • Plastic tubes (9)
  • Glue gun (1)
  • Saw (1)
  • Drill (1)
  • Valves (3)
  • Safety glasses (2)

Observations
  • I observed that by using enough water and hydraulic pressure, it can crush the objects
  • I also found that if there is an air bubble in the syringe, the system doesn't move all the syringes at the same time
  • The hydraulic press can crush a can to ⅓ of its original size

Recycling

Hydraulic presses make disposal and recycling of aluminum cans a lot more pleasant and even fun at the same time. It is a tool that can be used by everyone, starting from small kids to old people. It will not only free up space in your garbage bin or any other place you are storing empty aluminum cans, but will also help to recycle aluminum by doing good for the environment

Conclusion

When I applied pressure to the smaller syringe, then the bigger syringes magnified the force making it crush a soda can. This is just like our hypothesis where we assumed that the force would be magnified when the liquid is transferred from the small syringe to the bigger syringe.
